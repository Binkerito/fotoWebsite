Fond memories of film photographs

I was introduced to film photography from my early childhood years, wherever my family goes on a trip, my uncles always had cameras on them. I remember the moment I learnt the importance of film photography, unlike digital, once the shutter captures, that is it, there's no turning back or deleting the image. I learn this from the moment that I continously pushing the shutter trigger button to play with it before I knew how to use it, so then he explained how film rolls works and it should not be wasted.

First Photographer Ever

The world's first photograph—or at least the oldest surviving photo—was taken by Joseph Nicéphore Niépce in 1826. Captured using a technique known as heliography, the shot was taken from an upstairs window at Niépce's estate in Burgundy. As heliography produces one-of-a-kind images, there are no duplicates of the piece, which is now part of the permanent collection at the University of Texas-Austin.
